Chapter 2: Reminiscing Japan

Born in Japan but leaving at the age of five, I’ve always felt there’s something special about Japan, tucked away in the corners of my memory. Yet, those memories are like puzzle pieces, their edges blurred with time. Every time I visit Japan, once every two years, I find myself trying to piece them together, retracing the fragments of my childhood.

In the summer of 2023, I spent precious weeks with my family in Matsuyama, immersing myself in the simplicity and joy of everyday moments. Through my camera lens, I captured the unfiltered laughter and innocence of my Japanese cousins—their carefree smiles a reflection of the life I once lived. These fleeting moments of connection became a bridge between my present and the echoes of my childhood.

This chapter is not just a collection of photographs but a tapestry of nostalgia and reconnection—a visual diary of rediscovering Japan, my roots, and the warmth of family that weaves it all together.
