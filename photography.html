On the left is a portrait of me at the age of 18. Ever since I was a child, my mother would take me to a photography studio once every four years. I once asked her why this tradition mattered so much. She smiled and said, "Photography turns every portrait into a witness of time." She told me that one day, when I grew older, these photos would become cherished treasures, preserving memories of me, of us, and of the life we shared as a family.

As I reflect on her words, I realize how profoundly they shaped my love for photography. Through the lens of a camera, I’ve learned to see not just faces but emotions, not just scenes but stories. Photography has become my way of holding onto the fleeting beauty of life, a way of turning memories into something everlasting.

Here, I present Chapter 1 and Chapter 2—together, a love letter to my two hometowns.
